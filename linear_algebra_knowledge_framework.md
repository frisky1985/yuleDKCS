# Design Philosophy: Vector Space Architecture

## 1. Concept

A geometric meditation on the architecture of linearity — where vectors, matrices, and eigenvalues become luminous geometric presences on a deep void. The aesthetic bridges ancient Euclidean geometry and modern linear algebra: clean, precise, almost meditative. This is not a diagram; it is a map of mathematical space rendered as art.

## 2. Form & Composition

The knowledge tree flows left-to-right, echoing the natural learning progression: tools → structures → applications. Chapter nodes stand as vertical rectangular pillars; sub-concepts cluster beneath each pillar like coordinates in a basis. Ultra-fine connector lines trace the flow of ideas — horizontal for chapter-to-chapter progression, vertical for sub-concept hierarchy. Generous negative space creates a sense of breathing room, as though the concepts have room to exist without interference.

## 3. Color & Material

Five tones on a deep indigo-black field:
- **Void** (#0D0D1A): canvas ground — deep space for geometric thought
- **Ivory** (#F0EDE6): primary labels — warm, legible, authoritative
- **Amber Gold** (#D4A72C): major chapter nodes — vectors, geometry, light
- **Teal Cyan** (#2BBFA0): secondary accents — transformations, change
- **Violet** (#8B7EC8): tertiary accent — eigenvalues, eigenvectors, symmetry axes
- **Soft Grey** (#3A3A50): connector lines — recessive structure

No gradients. No shadows. Pure flat geometry, the way a mathematician thinks.

## 4. Typography & Text

Thin, modern sans-serif type at precise sizes. Chapter titles: 11–12pt light weight, uppercase for structural labels, title case for descriptive subtitles. Sub-concepts: 6.5–7.5pt light weight, single unified color. Letter-spacing calibrated to feel calm and systematic.

## 5. Scale & Rhythm

Two horizontal rows of 4 chapters each — 8 major pillars on a single canvas. Each chapter pillar: 7.2in wide, 4.8in tall with 0.55in horizontal gap. Sub-concepts flow beneath each pillar in columns of 3–5 items. The composition should feel inevitable — as though no element could be repositioned without disrupting the geometric harmony of the whole.

## 6. Knowledge Structure

**Row 1 — Foundations & Core Structures:**
1. Determinants — scalar essence of matrices
2. Matrices & Vectors — the basic language
3. Vector Spaces — abstract geometric structures
4. Linear Systems — solving in vector space

**Row 2 — Transformations & Advanced Applications:**
5. Linear Transformations — mappings between spaces
6. Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors — the axes of transformation
7. Inner Product Spaces — geometry in abstract spaces
8. Quadratic Forms — geometry of second-degree surfaces

**Knowledge Flow:** Determinants → Matrices → Vector Spaces → Linear Systems → Linear Transformations → Eigenvalues → Inner Products → Quadratic Forms
